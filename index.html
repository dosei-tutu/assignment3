Babies Are Surviving in NYS

Infant Mortality is on the Decline

By Demetria Osei-Tutu

December 16, 2021

In NYS, the infant mortality rate has declined. But in NYC, from 2002-2012 the rate has decreased by 23.2% while in NYS, it was only 10.5%. According to the CDC, infant mortality rates have decreased in urbanization. Neonatal births were higher in rural counties compared to urban counties. In research conducted by USF College of Public Health’s Dr. Russell Kirby along with colleagues Deborah Ehrenthal and Daphne Kuo, believes it has something to do with the greater social economic disadvantage in rural areas which leads to a lack of education pertaining to infants. Although, infant mortality is on the decline in NYS, disparities amongst infant mortality still persist. Its highest among Black babies.
